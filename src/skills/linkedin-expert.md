# LinkedIn Post Expert — Mobile-First Formatting

## FORMAT: LinkedIn Post (150-300 words)

### Structure
- **Line 1 (HOOK)**: This is the most important line. It appears before the "see more" fold on mobile. It must create immediate curiosity, tension, or a bold claim. If this line is boring, no one clicks. Test: would YOU stop scrolling for this?
- **Body**: Short paragraphs — 1-2 sentences MAX per paragraph. Build a narrative or argument. Include a personal angle — what you experienced, learned, or observed. Each paragraph should earn its line break.
- **Closer**: End with a question that invites real engagement (not "agree?" or "thoughts?"). Make people want to share their own experience or take a stance.

### Mobile-First Formatting Rules
- Line breaks between EVERY paragraph — wall of text = instant scroll-past on mobile
- No bullet points in the first 3 lines (LinkedIn truncates these below the fold)
- Keep paragraphs to 1-2 sentences — on a phone screen, 3 sentences looks like a wall
- No emoji spam — one or two strategic emojis max, or none
- First 2 lines must work standalone (they're all most people see)

### Hook Formulas That Work
1. **The counter-take**: "Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong."
2. **The confession**: "I [did something unexpected]. Here's what happened."
3. **The specific number**: "I [did X thing] for [Y time]. [Surprising result]."
4. **The pattern break**: Start mid-thought, as if the reader walked into a conversation

### Anti-Patterns (NEVER do these)
- "I'm humbled to announce" or any variation
- "I'm excited to share" — just share it
- Starting with "In today's [anything]"
- Hashtag spam at the bottom
- "Like and share if you agree"
- Tagging 20 people for engagement
- Generic motivational quotes without personal context
- "Let me know in the comments" without a specific question
- Long unbroken paragraphs — death on mobile
- Corporate jargon: "leveraging synergies", "thought leadership", "value proposition"

### Engagement Drivers
- Personal stories with specific details (not vague "a friend of mine")
- Contrarian takes backed by experience
- Frameworks or mental models people can immediately use
- Behind-the-scenes honesty about failures or challenges
- Questions that have no obvious right answer

### Tone
- Conversational, not corporate
- Confident, not arrogant
- Vulnerable when authentic, not performative
- Write like you're talking to one smart person, not broadcasting to thousands
