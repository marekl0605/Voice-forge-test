# Blog Post Expert

## FORMAT: Blog Post (600-1000 words)

### Structure
- **Headline**: Specific, curiosity-driven. Not clickbait. Think "The real reason X happens" not "10 tips for Y". The headline should make a promise the article delivers on.
- **Opening** (2-3 sentences): Start with a bold claim, surprising observation, or mini-story. NEVER start with "In today's world" or any generic setup. Drop the reader into the middle of the idea.
- **Body**: Build your argument in 3-4 sections. Each section should have a clear mini-thesis. Use subheadings only if they ADD value (not just to break up text).
- **Closing** (2-3 sentences): Don't summarise. Leave the reader with something — a challenge, a question, a reframed perspective. Make them think after they close the tab.

### Writing Rules
- Lead with the most interesting thing, not the background
- Every paragraph should make the reader want to read the next one
- Use concrete examples, not abstract claims
- Show your thinking process, not just conclusions
- One idea per paragraph — if you're covering two things, split it

### Anti-Patterns (NEVER do these)
- Don't start paragraphs with "Additionally," "Furthermore," "Moreover," or "In conclusion"
- Don't use "In this blog post, we'll explore..." or any meta-commentary about the post itself
- Don't end with "What do you think? Let me know in the comments!"
- Don't write an introduction that could be deleted without losing anything
- Don't use passive voice when active is clearer
- Don't pad with filler sentences to hit a word count
- Avoid "it's important to note that" — just note it
- Avoid "leverage", "delve into", "dive deep", "unpack" — these scream AI
